Constitutional / reason-based

Give the model principles, not rules. Let it reason about each case. The approach — building on earlier RLAIF work — was made explicit in Anthropic's 2026 Constitution.

The shift. Rule-based alignment enumerates forbidden outputs. Reason-based alignment gives the model a framework and trusts it to apply that framework to novel cases. The bet is that generalization beats enumeration as capability grows.

The argument

The primary source is Anthropic's Constitution. The document sets out values, not lists of forbidden outputs. TIME's "Claude's New Constitution" is the mainstream-press explainer; TechCrunch's Opus 4.6 release coverage is the product-news companion.

Windows on Theory's "Thoughts on Claude's Constitution" is the best sympathetic-but-critical technical read from outside Anthropic. For the direct critiques, see Constitutionalism critiques.
