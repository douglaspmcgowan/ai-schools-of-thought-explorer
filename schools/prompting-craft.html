Prompting craft

Specific techniques for specific models. Less "prompt engineering" as a category, more "here's the two-line trick that makes this particular model do the job." The taste-level stuff that isn't in the release notes.

Why this isn't generic. Models differ. What makes Codex behave isn't what makes Grok useful for search. This school values model-specific craft over universal prompting frameworks.

The argument

The canonical reference is OpenAI's Codex Prompting Guide — the model-makers' own documentation of what actually works on their coding-specialized frontier model. Required reading before doing anything serious with Codex 5.3; the advice does not transfer cleanly to other models.

On the other end of the spectrum, Ruben's Grok 420 is a specific technique for using Grok as a better search engine — the kind of practitioner trick that only works because the author has spent time with that model. Why Developers Keep Choosing Claude Over Every Other AI (Bhusal) makes the complementary case — what Claude specifically does well, from a developer who has compared.

The research-side reality check is Tencent's Learning from context is harder than we thought — a reminder that some prompting tricks rest on shakier ground than practitioners assume. Read it alongside the cookbooks, not instead of them.
