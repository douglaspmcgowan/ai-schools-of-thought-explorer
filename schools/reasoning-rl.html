Reasoning + RL mechanics

Why chain-of-thought plus reinforcement learning is the current engine of progress. And the pushback: word models aren't world models, and RL on text may hit a wall text alone can't push through.

Why this matters for other debates. The AGI timelines and bubble-or-not arguments both load-bear on claims about reasoning RL. If the mechanism really is self-improving (AlphaZero-style), extrapolators win; if word models plateau, doubters win.

The argument

Reinforcement Learning for LLMs (mesuvash) is the accessible mechanical explainer — how RL signals actually shape what a reasoning model does at training time, and why this is different from the pre-2024 pretraining-only paradigm. It's the best single piece to read before any of the timeline essays.

The research-paper version is Reinforcement World Model Learning for LLM Agents (arXiv, 2026) — an attempt to give agents an explicit world model via RL rather than hoping it emerges from text. Directly relevant to the LLM-paradigm-doubter critique that word models can't substitute for world models.

On the "why this isn't a bubble" side, Matt Honnibal's Why I don't think AI is a bubble is the sharpest argument from mechanics (not hype) — reasoning RL plus longer chains of thought means no human-data bottleneck, and recognizing good paths is easier than generating them, so self-improvement loops work. See the not-a-bubble school for full treatment.
