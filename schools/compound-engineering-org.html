Compound engineering org

Stack specialized AI roles — planner, builder, reviewer, shipper — behind each human. The company chart mirrors the agent pipeline. Every's own operating model: 25 people, four headline AI agents, every IC partnered with a stack.

The difference from "AI-native flat." Flat orgs assume the AI makes humans more interchangeable. Compound-engineering orgs assume the AI makes humans more specialized, because each human's agents are the accumulation of how they work.

The argument

Every is the primary source for this school, and the defining essay is their own How We Run a 25-Person Company on Four AI Agents (2026-04-09). The pipeline idea is a direct lift from the compound engineering coding practice, scaled to the org chart. What I Learned Onboarding Our AI Project Manager (2026-03-31) is the concrete stage-by-stage account. Seven Things I've Learned Getting Companies to Use AI (2026-03-30) is the generalization attempt — what travels from Every's setup to other orgs.

Tom Tunguz's "9 Observations on Building with AI Agents" reads the pattern from the investor side. The "Building an Elite AI Engineering Culture in 2026" essay (CJ Roth) is the culture-side piece.
