Extrapolators

Draw the capability line forward. If it's been straight for five years, bet on five more. The extrapolators don't claim to know the mechanism — they claim the curves have been predictive enough to be load-bearing for planning.

The move. Take the published benchmark trajectories, extend them, and treat the extension as actionable. The school includes frontier-lab CEOs (Amodei, Altman, Hassabis) but its cleanest expositors are usually outside the labs.

The argument

Samuel Hammond's "Empiricists vs. Extrapolators" is the defining framing essay. Hammond is an extrapolator by his own typology: the capability line has been straight enough, long enough, that betting against it requires more evidence than betting with it. Leopold Aschenbrenner's "Situational Awareness" essay is often cited as the maximal version of this argument — the public hasn't fully internalized what the trend lines imply.

The school gets public reinforcement from frontier-lab CEOs. Dave Friedman's writeup of Jensen Huang's commentary on Anthropic and OpenAI captures the infrastructure-side version: the compute buildout assumes the line keeps going. Citadel's "2026 Global Intelligence Crisis" is the market-side version.

See Empiricists for the direct counter-position; LLM-paradigm doubters for a deeper critique that calls the trend line an artifact of benchmark design.
