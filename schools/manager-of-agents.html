Manager of agents

The IC skill set pivots from "do the work" to "direct agents doing the work." Delegating, reviewing, handling escalations, noticing when an agent is stuck — these were management skills. Now they're IC skills too.

Why it's its own school. It's possible to run a compound-engineering pipeline where humans write specs and agents execute; that's spec-driven thinking applied to org design. Manager-of-agents is a different claim: the core skill itself is management, not specification.

The argument

Every's You're the Manager Now (2026-04-16) names this school most directly. Related pieces from Every build out the manager-of-agents worldview: How to Design for Human-Agent Interaction (2026-04-03) is the interface-design half; The Missing Layer in AI Adoption (2026-04-12) identifies the manager-of-agents competency as the bottleneck to enterprise rollouts.

The career-side companion is CEO Dinner's "Why IQ and EQ Aren't Enough Anymore: The Age of AI Demands AQ" — the claim that a new skill (roughly: agentic quotient, the ability to manage AI workers) is the emerging differentiator. "Design Careers in the Age of AI: Specialize or Generalize?" is the practitioner-facing career piece.
