Sidekick for everyone

Every human in the company gets a personal agent, wired into their specific workflow. Not shared infrastructure — personal infrastructure. The agent compounds with the human's own working style, and it goes with them if they leave.

Contrast with compound-engineering orgs. That school stacks agents behind pipeline stages. The sidekick school stacks agents beside individual humans. Organizationally, this keeps the human as the primary unit and treats agents as the extension of that unit.

The argument

Every's Everyone Gets a Sidekick (2026-03-29) names the pattern. The related product piece, Introducing Plus One: One-Click OpenClaw Agents (2026-03-26), is Every's attempt to productize it. Build Your Own Bloomberg Terminal With AI (2026-03-27) is the user-side account of what a sufficiently-personal sidekick becomes.

The operational companion writing fills out what it looks like day-to-day: House Rules for the Agents (2026-04-05) — how personal sidekicks get governed — and Every Is Half Agent Now (2026-04-08) — the etiquette when sidekicks mediate communication between humans. The sidekick school overlaps with folder-as-agent at the technical substrate level: each person's sidekick is, in Every's implementation, a folder they own.
