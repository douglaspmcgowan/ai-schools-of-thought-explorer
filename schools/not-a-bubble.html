Not a bubble

The valuations price in real progress because progress is real. Reasoning-RL, not pre-training scaling, is what's still compounding — and the bubble-skeptics haven't reckoned with it.

Pro-progress but grounded. This isn't a "number go up" argument. It's a mechanism argument: reasoning-RL + longer chains of thought + no human-text bottleneck = self-improvement loops that the AlphaZero analogy predicted.

The argument

Matt Honnibal's "Why I don't think AI is a bubble" is the school's cleanest defense. Honnibal (computational linguist, co-founder of spaCy / Explosion AI) argues that plateau-predictions assume pure pre-training scaling — which has indeed slowed — while missing the separate compounding regime of reasoning-RL. The AlphaZero analogy is load-bearing: recognizing good reasoning paths is easier than generating them, so self-improvement loops work. No public reason to believe progress halts.

Nathan Lambert's "Opus 4.6, Codex 5.3, and the post-benchmark era" (Interconnects) is the "what the next phase looks like" companion. Tom Tunguz's "9 Observations on Building with AI Agents" is the investor-side read of which results are real vs. marketing. Citadel Securities' "2026 Global Intelligence Crisis" is the market-maker-side view that the capex is rational given expected capability.

Counter-positions: bubble-skeptics (the productivity-statistics reading), and the No product camp, which accepts capability is real but doubts defensible businesses.
