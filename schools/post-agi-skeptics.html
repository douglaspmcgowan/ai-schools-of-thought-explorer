Post-AGI skeptics

Maybe "AGI" is a rhetorical device, not a destination. The milestone keeps moving because it was never a milestone. The skeptics ask: what if the term is functioning as a fundraising tool?

What makes this distinct. Other skeptical schools argue about mechanism or evidence. Post-AGI skeptics argue about the category — that "AGI" doesn't carve reality at a useful joint, and using it as an organizing concept leads to bad policy and bad investment.

The argument

The American Prospect's "What If There's No AGI?" is the school's clearest public statement. The argument is not that progress will stop; it's that the concept "AGI" is load-bearing in valuations, policy debates, and safety arguments, and if the concept dissolves, all three unravel in interesting ways.

Anthony Noided's "I guess I kinda get why people hate AI" is the cultural-reception companion — a practitioner explaining why the AGI framing has become alienating to people who otherwise use AI tools daily. Greater Wrong's career-decision piece, "Productivity: The Evidence Is Mixed, But the Pattern Is Clear", is the rationalist-adjacent version — taking AGI seriously as a framing while noting the productivity evidence doesn't support current urgency.

Counter-position: Extrapolators, who treat AGI as a meaningful forecast target.
